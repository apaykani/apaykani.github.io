## Working in the group

We are a computational group with an experimental edge, working on problems where combustion physics and machine learning genuinely need each other. Members typically develop strong skills in reacting-flow CFD, chemical kinetics, and modern machine-learning frameworks, and publish in venues such as *Combustion and Flame*, *Proceedings of the Combustion Institute*, *Fuel* and *Applied Thermal Engineering*.

## PhD students

I supervise PhD students through QMUL's School of Engineering and Materials Science. Funded positions are advertised here and on FindAPhD as they arise.

I am also glad to support strong applicants to competitive schemes — QMUL Principal's Postgraduate Research Studentships, the China Scholarship Council, and national scholarship programmes. If you are considering this route, please contact me at least **three months** before the relevant deadline, since a competitive application takes time to develop.

## Postdoctoral researchers

Funded PDRA positions are advertised on the QMUL jobs portal and listed above. I am happy to support applications to fellowship schemes including:

- Royal Society Newton International Fellowship
- Marie Skłodowska-Curie Postdoctoral Fellowships
- EPSRC Postdoctoral Fellowships
- Leverhulme Early Career Fellowship
- Royal Academy of Engineering Research Fellowship

## Visiting researchers

We host visiting PhD students and academics for periods of three to twelve months. Visitors are expected to arrive with their own funding.

## How to get in touch

Email me at [a.paykani@qmul.ac.uk](mailto:a.paykani@qmul.ac.uk) with:

1. A brief statement of your research interests
2. Your CV
3. **Which of our four [research themes]({{ '/research/' | relative_url }}) you want to work in, and why**

The third point matters. Generic enquiries are not usually answered.
